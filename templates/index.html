About the Campaign

What is Deep Sea Mining?

Deep sea mining involves extracting valuable minerals from the ocean floor, often using heavy machinery that destroys fragile ecosystems.

This industry threatens marine life, disrupts carbon storage, and risks irreversible damage to our planet's last untouched frontier.

Why Should We Care?

The deep sea is home to unique species and plays a critical role in regulating Earth's climate. Mining could destroy these ecosystems forever.

By stopping deep sea mining, we protect biodiversity, combat climate change, and safeguard the ocean for future generations.

The Impact of Deep Sea Mining

Destruction of Habitats

Mining operations destroy seafloor habitats, wiping out species that have evolved over millions of years.

Climate Change

The deep sea stores vast amounts of carbon. Mining disrupts this process, accelerating climate change.
